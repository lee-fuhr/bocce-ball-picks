How to choose the right bocce ball set

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best bocce ball set for most people balances weight, surface compatibility, and portability. Start with an 8-ball set of 100mm or 107mm balls weighing around 2 pounds each, they work well on grass, sand, and hard courts. Avoid ultra-cheap sets with hollow plastic balls, as they won’t roll true and won’t last a season.

What size bocce ball set should I buy?

Most recreational sets come with eight balls, four per team, plus a pallino (the small target ball) and a measuring device. For casual backyard or beach play, a 100mm or 107mm ball diameter is the sweet spot. 100mm balls are slightly lighter and easier to control for kids or beginners, while 107mm balls feel more substantial and score better on uneven grass. If you plan to play on a regulation court, the standard ball size is 107mm (about 4.2 inches) and weighs roughly 2 pounds. Serious clubs and tournaments use these dimensions. For pure fun at a picnic or campsite, a smaller set around 90mm still works, but you’ll notice the difference in rolling consistency the moment the ground gets bumpy. Avoid sets with massive size variability within the same box, mismatched diameters make fair play nearly impossible. Stick with well-known brands like GoSports or Regent, which offer consistent sizes and reliable resin or composite material.

How does ball weight affect bocce play on different surfaces?

Heavier balls (around 2 pounds or more) cut through thick grass and hold their line better on windy days or uneven terrain. Lighter balls bounce erratically and can be pushed off course by a stiff breeze. For soft grass or sand, lean toward the heavier end of the spectrum, you want the ball to settle rather than skid. On hard surfaces like packed clay or a dedicated bocce court, mid-weight balls (1.8 to 2.1 pounds) give you a nice balance of control and speed. Very light balls (under 1.5 pounds) are fine for a casual toss on a flat lawn, but they won’t deliver the satisfying roll that makes the game addictive. A good rule of thumb: if you can easily palm the ball and it feels solid without being tiring to throw 20 times in a row, the weight is right for your needs. Most well-regarded sets land in the 2-pound range.

What materials are best for bocce balls?

Resin is the modern standard: it’s dense, durable, and provides a consistent roll. Many top-rated sets use a solid resin core with a smooth finish. Some budget sets use hollow plastic or filled shells, which dent, crack, or become unbalanced after a few games. Avoid those if you want the set to last more than one season. Metal sets (usually aluminum or steel) exist but are rare and expensive, they’re overkill for casual play. Wooden balls are traditional but warp or split with moisture. Resin is the sweet spot: it resists chips, handles sun and humidity, and gives a satisfying thud when balls collide. Check that the balls are “solid” resin, not blow-molded plastic. You can tell by weight: hollow balls feel noticeably lighter. Reputable brands like GoSports or Regent specifically market their sets as “solid resin.”

Is a carrying case really necessary?

If you plan to bring your bocce set to the beach, park, or a friend’s house, a padded carry case (or at least a sturdy bag) is worth it. Without one, balls rattle against each other and the pallino can get lost. Many sets come with a zippered tote or a sling-style bag, look for one with separate compartments or foam dividers. A good case also keeps sand and dirt out of the ball surface, which helps maintain a smooth roll. If the set you’re eyeing doesn’t include a bag, budget for a simple mesh or canvas tote. It’s one of those small upgrades that saves you from hunting for a loose pallino under a picnic blanket.

Should I buy a portable or a tournament-grade set?

Portable sets (under 15 pounds total) are perfect for tailgating, camping, and backyard fun. They usually come in a compact bag and use resin or composite balls around 100–107mm. These are the sets you’ll see on Amazon with thousands of positive reviews, they strike the right balance of quality and convenience. Tournament-grade sets are heavier (balls closer to 2.5 pounds), often made of polished resin with precise weight and diameter tolerances. They also include a regulation 10-foot measuring device and a regulation pallino. If you’re joining a league or hosting serious matches, invest in a tournament set. For everyone else, a good portable set is far more practical and won’t break the bank. A common mistake is buying a super cheap set that claims “regulation size” but uses brittle plastic, save yourself the frustration and skip anything under about a few dozen dollars.

Frequently asked questions

What is the standard number of balls in a bocce set?

A regulation set includes eight balls (four per team) and one pallino. Some recreational sets include two pallinos for backup, but eight is the standard for play.

Are all bocce balls the same size?

No, sizes range from about 90mm up to 110mm in diameter. 100mm and 107mm are the most common for casual and league play, respectively. The pallino is typically 60mm.

Can I use bocce balls on grass, sand, and hard courts?

Yes, quality resin balls work on all three, though performance varies. Heavier balls roll better on grass and sand; on hard courts, lighter balls can skid. Adjust your throwing weight accordingly.

What is the difference between yard bocce and professional bocce?

Yard bocce uses slightly smaller, lighter balls (often 90–100mm) and a shorter court length. Professional bocce uses regulation 107mm balls, a 60mm pallino, and a court roughly 76 feet long. The throwing technique and strategy are the same, but the equipment and court dimensions differ.

How do I clean and maintain my bocce ball set?

Wipe the balls with a damp cloth after play to remove dirt and sand. Dry them thoroughly before storing in the case. Avoid leaving them in direct sunlight for long stretches, as UV rays can fade or degrade resin over time.

In shortThe right bocce ball set makes the difference between a fun afternoon and a frustrating one. Stick with solid resin balls around 100–107mm, skip the ultra-cheap hollow options, and make sure you have a proper carrying case. Invest a little more up front and you’ll have a set that rolls true for years of backyard tournaments.
