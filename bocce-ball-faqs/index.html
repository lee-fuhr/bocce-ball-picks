Bocce ball questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best bocce ball set for you depends on where you play, how often you play, and your budget. Focus on ball weight, material (resin vs. metal), and whether you need a full 8-ball set with a pallino and measuring device.

What should I look for in a bocce ball set?

Start with the number of balls. Most regulation sets include eight balls (four per team) plus a pallino (small target ball) and a measuring device. If you are buying for casual backyard fun, a four-ball set for two players works, but an eight-ball set is better for full teams. Ball weight is essential. Official bocce balls weigh between 900 and 1100 grams (about 2 to 2.4 pounds). Heavier balls hold their line better on uneven grass or sand, while lighter balls are easier to carry but can be pushed around by wind or bumps. Choose a weight that matches the surface you play on most often.

How many balls come in a regulation bocce set?

Regulation bocce sets contain eight balls: four of one color and four of another. Two teams of up to four players each use these. Along with the eight balls, the set includes a pallino (also called the jack) and a measuring tape or string to determine which ball is closest. Some recreational sets come with only four balls (two per team) for quick games with two or three people. If you plan to play with larger groups, look for an eight-ball set. Many sets also include a carrying case or bag, which keeps everything organized.

Is resin or metal better for bocce balls?

Resin balls are the most common choice for everyday players. They are durable, affordable, and available in many colors. High-quality resin balls have a dense, consistent core that rolls true. They work well on grass, dirt, and sand courts. Metal balls, typically made of steel or aluminum, are less common and primarily used on hard, flat surfaces like concrete or asphalt. Metal balls are heavier and do not have the same lively bounce as resin. They also scratch more easily. For most recreational play, resin is the practical pick because it offers good grip and lasts for years with minimal care.

How do I choose the right bocce ball size?

Regulation bocce balls have a standard diameter of about 107 millimeters (just over 4 inches). Some sets offer slightly smaller balls (around 100 mm) for children or travel versions. If you plan to play official games, stick to the 107 mm size so the balls feel consistent in your hand. Weight also ties into size. Most adults prefer balls in the 900-gram to 1000-gram range. Heavier balls (close to 1100 grams) give more control on soft surfaces but can tire your arm over an afternoon. Try lifting a few sets in person if possible, or read owner reviews that mention handling on grass versus hard courts.

What accessories do I really need for bocce?

A measuring device is the most important accessory after the balls themselves. A simple string or tape with a metal end lets you check which ball is closest to the pallino without squinting or crouching. Many sets include one, but if yours does not, a simple retractable measuring tape works fine. A carrying bag or case helps you transport the set to the park or beach. Some sets come with a canvas tote or a hard case. Look for one with separate pockets for the pallino and measuring device so nothing gets lost. Chalk or tape to mark court boundaries is optional but useful for serious games.

Can I play bocce on grass or sand?

Yes, bocce is traditionally played on grass, but it also works on packed sand (like a beach volleyball court) or fine gravel. On grass, the balls roll slower and the surface can affect direction if the grass is thick or uneven. On sand, balls tend to stop more quickly. Courts made of crushed oyster shell or clay are common in clubs but not necessary for casual play. If you mostly play on grass, choose a heavier ball set (around 1000 grams) so the ball cuts through the turf. For sand or hard courts, a mid-weight ball (900–950 grams) offers a good balance of control and distance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the pallino or jack in bocce?

The pallino (also called the jack or boccino) is a small white ball, about half the size of the bocce balls. Players throw the pallino to start each frame, then try to roll their bocce balls as close to it as possible. The pallino is often included in every standard set.

How much does a good bocce ball set cost?

Prices vary widely by brand and quality. You can find decent recreational sets for while premium tournament-grade sets can cost a few hundred dollars. Mid-range sets in the range usually offer a good balance of weight consistency, durability, and color options.

What is the official court size for bocce?

Regulation bocce courts are 90 feet long by 13 feet wide. However, most backyard players adapt to whatever space they have. A typical lawn game can be played on a patch of grass about 50 to 70 feet long. The key is to have clear boundaries so balls don’t roll too far away.

How do I know if a bocce set is tournament-level?

Tournament sets usually meet World Bocce Federation standards: eight balls with a diameter of 107 mm and a weight of 920 grams or more. They are made from high-density resin with a uniform feel. The balls will have a manufacturer’s weight class marked on them. If the set feels lightweight or the colors are painted on rather than molded in, it is likely recreational, not tournament-grade.

Can children or seniors play bocce easily?

Absolutely. Bocce is a low-impact game that people of all ages and abilities can enjoy. Look for sets with lighter balls (around 800–900 grams) if you have kids or older players who may have trouble with heavy balls. Some sets also include smaller balls for young children. The rules are simple, and the game moves at a relaxed pace.

How do I maintain my bocce set?

Resin balls need minimal care. Wipe them with a damp cloth after playing on dirt or sand, and store them in a cool, dry place out of direct sunlight. Avoid leaving them in a hot car, as extreme heat can warp the resin. Metal balls should be dried thoroughly after use to prevent rust. Check the carrying case regularly for holes so nothing gets lost.

In shortThe best bocce ball set is the one you will actually take to the park, beach, or backyard. Prioritize a full eight-ball set in a weight that feels comfortable for your typical playing surface, and don’t skimp on a measuring tool. With a well-selected set, you will be ready for hours of friendly competition with family and friends.
